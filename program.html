Weekly Themes

Five artist-inspired weeks with enough structure to teach and enough looseness to make camp feel magical.

The summer is built around five different worlds. Families can choose one week, multiple weeks, or ask about drop-ins if joining late.

Week 1 • Matisse

Color Lab / Color & Joy

Color theory, giant painted wheels, shape collage, pattern design, collaborative mural, and wearable art.
Week 2 • O'Keeffe

Nature Lab / Nature & Texture

Observational drawing, flowers up close, texture rubbings, leaf impressions, mobiles, and driftwood sculptures.
Week 3 • Basquiat

Street Art / Self Expression

Graffiti lettering, symbolism, cityscapes, poetry posters, layering, and giant collaborative wall work.
Week 4 • Dali

Invention Lab / Dreams & Surrealism

Dream journals, surreal self portraits, clay creatures, fantasy room boxes, transformed objects, and puppet theater.
Week 5 • Picasso

The Big Art Studio

Abstract faces, cardboard engineering, giant collaborative canvas, choice-day making, and exhibition design.

Daily Flow

Parents need the structure to be clear. Kids need the day to feel alive. The camp schedule is built to do both.

9:00-9:30
Arrival and sketchbook warm-up.
9:30-10:00
Artist lesson and slideshow.
10:00-11:30
Main project block.
11:30-12:00
Snack and outdoor play.
12:00-1:00
Second creative activity.
1:00-1:30
Reflection and journaling.
1:30-2:00
Cleanup and gallery share.

All-Summer Threads

These recurring rituals make the season feel like one connected creative world rather than disconnected signups.

Artist Passports

Campers collect one stamp per artist week and build a sense of continuity across the summer.

Collaborative Mural

One mural builds all season long, so even drop-in campers can contribute to something larger.

Parent Shares

Every Friday closes with work on view, making the camp visible to families and meaningful to kids.

Joining late?

Drop-ins and partial weeks are available when capacity allows. We will place campers in weeks where the projects still work well as a late entry point.
